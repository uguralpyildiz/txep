Our commitment to come up with new technologies to producing electricity and pure water for everyone and everywhere.

Texep Ltd is committed to protecting the climate and the environment. Texep invents, develops and implements innovative solutions for the production and storage of renewable energy as well as the desalination and purification of water.

Texep builds on and extends a 45-year company history. The entrepreneurial spirit of two generations, father Mario and son André Posnansky, led to the realization of pioneering projects through the companies they founded and managed: Solaire France SARL, Solaire Suisse Ltd and Sunstyle Ltd.

Solaire France SARL

SOLAIRE FRANCE was founded in 2007 by André and Mario Posnansky with the purpose of development, financing, and turnkey realization of the Saint Charles Solaire power plant in Perpignan, France. The Saint Charles Solaire plant, realized in 2008–2011, with a capacity of 9 megawatts, an area of 70,000 m² and 97,000 installed solar shingles, is to date the largest building-integrated solar power plant in the world. Today, TEXEP is the parent company of SOLAIRE FRANCE with a stake in the Saint Charles Solaire power plant.

Solaire Suisse LTD

As a general contractor, SOLAIRE SUISSE Ltd realised a large number of solar projects on a turnkey basis with the SUNSTYLE cladding system between 2007 and 2015. With SOLAIRE SUISSE Ltd, private and business customers as well as farmers received an "all from one hand" solution, which ensured the highest quality level from fist consultation to the completed project. At the same time, SOLAIRE SUISSE Ltd created unique and groundbreaking references, which subsequently enabled the SUNSTYLE solar roof to be widely used. In 2015, SOLAIRE SUSSE Ltd was renamed SUNSTYLE Ltd, which to this day focuses exclusively on the production and distribution of the SUNSTYLE cladding system to sales partners in Switzerland and abroad.

Sunstyle LTD

SUNSTYLE Ltd was majority-owned and managed by André Posnansky for 15 years, developed the patented electricity-producing solar roof of the same name, SUNSTYLE®, and realized a number of outstanding projects on the basis of the technology. SUNSTYLE Ltd was sold in 2021. Convinced by the product, TEXEP continues to use the SUNSTYLE® solar roof system for building-integrated photovoltaics.

Google Campus in California

SUNSTYLE developed the active building envelope of the two Google campuses in close collaboration with Google, the architects of Heatherwick Studio and BIG (Bjarke Ingels Group). The solar roof system was enhanced to be able to cover curved roof surfaces. The use of special glass to avoid reflections and the development of a color technology for individual coloring of the solar tiles in cooperation with Saint Gobain (Germany) and Solvis (Croatia) met the aesthetic and technical requirements of the client. The project also included the manufacture and supply of over 90,000 active and inactive shingles and associated fastening components. These advanced solar shingles were used on the canopy-like roof of Google's Charleston East and Bay View campuses in California.
